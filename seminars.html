Seminar Highlights

Saturday, November 14
Saturday morning program is scheduled from 9 a.m.-12:30 p.m., followed by a sit-down lunch lasting until 3 p.m. David Savona, executive editor, Cigar Aficionado and Gordon Mott, senior contributing editor, Cigar Aficionado, are the co-moderators for the weekend program.

9:00 – 9:30 a.m. • Cigar Aficionado's Top Three Cigars of 2014 Tasting. The top 25 cigars of 2014 were selected by our senior editors and announced on the Cigar Aficionado website in December 2014. When you enter, you will be given a packet containing the No. 1, No. 2 and No. 3 cigars of 2014. At each of the morning cigar breaks, we will designate one cigar to smoke. Each is presented by the cigarmaker onstage. At this first cigar break we smoke Cigar Aficionado’s No. 3 cigar of 2014, Illusione Fume d’Amour Clementes presented by Dion Giolito, owner, Illusione Cigars.

9:30 – 10:05 a.m. • Cuba Update. An in-depth look into what’s new in Cuba with an emphasis on cigars, of course. Gordon Mott and David Savona will lead the discussion with Hirochi Robaina, a renowned Cuban tobacco grower.

10:05 – 10:25 a.m. • Second Cigar Break, No. 2 Cigar. In this cigar break, Ernesto Perez-Carrillo, EPC Cigar Co., presents Cigar Aficionado’s No. 2 cigar of 2014, E.P. Carrillo La Historia E-III.

10:25 – 11:05 a.m. • Mysteries of Maduro Cigars. A look into the world of increasingly popular dark cigars, especially those wrapped in Connecticut broadleaf wrappers. Our panel of tobacco experts includes Larry Palombo, Altadis USA; Ernest Gocaj, General Cigar Co. and Willy Herrera, Drew Estate. The discussion is led by Gregory Mottola, senior editor, Cigar Aficionado.

11:05 a.m. – 11:30 a.m. • Humidity and Your Cigars. Panelists Daniel Marshall, D. Marshall Humidors, and Sean Knudsen, Boveda Humidification Systems offer tips on maintaining proper humidity in your humidor which is essential to cigar aging. Hosted by David Savona and Andrew Nagy, associate editor.

11:30 – 11:55 a.m. • Third Cigar Break, No. 1 Cigar. At this third cigar break we smoke Cigar Aficionado’s No. 1 cigar of 2014, Oliva Serie V Melanio Figurado. Onstage is Jose Oliva, Oliva Cigar Co. to discuss his cigar.

11:55 a.m. – 12:30 p.m. • 20 Years of Fuente Fuente OpusX. This year marks the 20th anniversary of the Fuente Fuente OpusX cigar. Carlos Fuente Jr. speaks about the birth and evolution of this iconic cigar.

12:30 - 3:00 p.m. Lunch with La Flor Dominicana. Enjoy a delicious seated lunch hosted by Litto Gomez and his La Flor Dominicana team. There will be cigars for everyone and plenty of time for a Q & A session.

Sunday, November 15
Sunday’s program from 9:30 a.m.-1:00 p.m. consists of three cigar seminars on a lighter note … involving a hands-on approach. Each is led by an expert.

9:30 – 10:30 a.m. • Cigar Lover's Breakfast. Start the day with a real Cigar Aficionado’s breakfast. Chef Nicole Brisson from CarneVino will create a breakfast menu specifically designed for this cigar-loving crowd. She will share some of her tales from the kitchen. Of course, we will find the perfect cigar to match this breakfast.

10:45 – 11:45 a.m. • Roll Your Own, Seminar No. 18. Michael Giannini and Leo Peraza of General Cigar Co. bring everything you need to create your own cigar including real tobacco as used in a premium cigar factory. Follow along with one of La Gloria Cubana’s most experienced premium cigar makers. Whether you’ve done this before or you are a new roller, everyone enjoys the opportunity to roll and create their own cigar and perhaps win a prize.

12:00 – 1:00 p.m. • Bourbon for Certain! What’s the hottest call at the whiskey bar today? America’s native spirit, Bourbon. Cigar Aficionado’s spirits editor Jack Bettridge will demonstrate why Bourbon is a universal donor in a pairing with fine cigars.
