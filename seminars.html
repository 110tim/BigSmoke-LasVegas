Weekend Package

Saturday and Sunday daytime hours will

Seminar Highlights

Saturday, November 8
Saturday morning program is scheduled from 9 a.m.-12:25 p.m., followed by a sit-down lunch lasting until 3 p.m. Gordon Mott, executive editor, Cigar Aficionado and David Savona, senior editor, Cigar Aficionado are the co-moderators for the weekend program.

9 – 9:40 a.m. • Cigar Aficionado's Top Three Legal Cigars of 2013 Tasting. The top 25 cigars of 2013 were selected by our senior editors and announced on the Cigar Aficionado website in January 2014. When you enter, you will be given a packet containing the No. 2, No. 3 and No. 4 cigars of 2013. Cigar No. 1 is not legal. At each of the morning cigar breaks, we will designate one cigar to smoke. Each is presented by the cigarmaker onstage. At this first cigar break we smoke Cigar Aficionado's No. 4 cigar of 2013, Padrón 1964 Anniversary Series Diplomatico Maduro presented by Jorge Padrón and José Orlando Padrón.

9:40 – 10:15 a.m. • "Fat Cigar" Trends. Are you one of the lovers of the "fat cigar?"" An in-depth look into today’s unique shape phenomenon. On our panel of cigarmakers are Matt Booth, Room 101 Cigars, Ernesto Perez-Carrillo Jr., EPC Cigar Co. and Michael Herklots, Nat Sherman. Gordon Mott and David Savona lead the discussion.

10:15 – 10:45 a.m. • Second Cigar Break, No. 3 Cigar. In this cigar break, Davidoff's Richard Krutick, Director of Marketing and Jeff Stone, US Brand Ambassador, present Cigar Aficionado's No. 3 cigar of 2013 the Davidoff Nicaragua Toro.

10:45 – 11:20 a.m. • Different Tobacco Varietals. A look into the many tobacco varietals used by the cigar industry to blend premium cigars. Our panel of tobacco experts includes Eduardo Fernandez, Casa Fernandez, Litto Gomez, La Flor Dominicana, and Manuel (Manolo) Quesada, Quesada Cigars. The discussion is led by Gregory Mottola, senior editor, Cigar Aficionado.

11:20 – 11:50 a.m. • Third Cigar Break, No. 2 Cigar. At this third cigar break we smoke Cigar Aficionado's No. 2 cigar of 2013, Aging Room Quattro F55 Concerto. Onstage is Rafael Nodal, owner, Boutique Blend Cigars who will discuss his cigar.

11:50 a.m. – 12:25 p.m. • Unique Tobacco Farms and Valleys. Gordon Mott and David Savona explore the three most distinctive tobacco growing areas. From Mexico, Alejandro Turrent, A. Turrent Cigars, from Dominican Republic, Carlos Fuente Jr., Tabacalera A. Fuente and from Nicaragua, Rocky Patel, Rocky Patel Premium Cigars.

12:25 – 3:00 p.m. • Lunch with Alec Bradley Cigars. Enjoy a delicious seated lunch hosted by Alan Rubin, Alec Bradley Cigars and the rest of his team. They will greet you and share some of their cigar experiences. In this open forum, you can ask questions or comment. There will be cigars for everyone.

Sunday, November 9
Sunday’s program from 9:30 a.m.-1:00 p.m. consists of three cigar seminars on a lighter note … involving a hands-on approach. Each is led by an expert.

9:30 – 10:30 a.m. • Cigar Lovers Breakfast. Start the day with a real cigar aficionado’s breakfast. Chef Nicole Brisson from CarneVino will create a breakfast menu specifically designed for this cigar-loving crowd. She will share some of her tales from the kitchen. Of course, we will find the perfect cigar to match this breakfast.

10:45 – 11:45 a.m. • Roll Your Own, Seminar No. 17. Michael Giannini and Leo Peraza of General Cigar Co. bring everything you need to create your own cigar including real tobacco as used in a premium cigar factory. Follow along with one of La Gloria Cubana’s most experienced premium cigar makers. Whether you've done this before or you are a new roller, everyone enjoys the opportunity to roll and create their own cigar and perhaps win a prize.

12:00 noon – 1:00 p.m. • Canadian Accents The smooth—style whisky of our neighbors to the north will be the focus of this year's spirits-and-cigar pairing. Led by Cigar Aficionado’s drinks guru Jack Bettridge, we'll taste a few of each and discover why border relations are so good.
